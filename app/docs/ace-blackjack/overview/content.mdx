# ACE Blackjack Overview

ACE is a precision-engineered, RNG-audited blackjack game platform built for enterprise iGaming. This section covers game mechanics, rules, and player actions.

## Game Rules

ACE follows traditional casino blackjack rules with the following specifications:

### Card Values
- **2-10:** Face value (2 = 2 points, 10 = 10 points)
- **Face Cards (J, Q, K):** 10 points each
- **Ace:** 1 or 11 points (player choice in certain game states)

### Win Conditions

1. **Blackjack (Natural):** Player receives an Ace and a 10-value card on initial deal (21 with 2 cards)
   - Payout: 3:2 (1.5x bet)
   - Beats all other hands except dealer blackjack

2. **Player 21:** Player reaches 21 without busting
   - Payout: 1:1 (equal bet)

3. **Dealer Bust:** Dealer's hand exceeds 21 while player's hand is ≤21
   - Payout: 1:1 (equal bet)

4. **Higher Hand:** Player's hand is higher than dealer's without exceeding 21
   - Payout: 1:1 (equal bet)

5. **Push (Tie):** Player and dealer have the same hand value
   - Payout: 0 (bet returned)

### Loss Conditions

1. **Player Bust:** Player's hand exceeds 21
   - Loss: Full bet amount

2. **Dealer Wins:** Dealer's hand is higher than player's and doesn't bust
   - Loss: Full bet amount

3. **Dealer Blackjack:** Dealer has 21 with 2 cards (only if player doesn't have blackjack)
   - Loss: Full bet amount

## Game Flow

### Phase 1: Betting
- Player places initial wager
- Bet amount must be within table limits

### Phase 2: Deal
- Both player and dealer receive 2 cards
- Player's cards are face-up
- Dealer shows 1 card (upcard) and hides 1 card (hole card)

### Phase 3: Player Actions
- Player decides action based on hand value and dealer upcard
- Actions remain available until player stands or busts

### Phase 4: Dealer Play
- Dealer reveals hole card
- Dealer must hit on 16 or less (soft 17 rule)
- Dealer must stand on 17 or more

### Phase 5: Outcome Resolution
- Compare final hands
- Apply appropriate payout
- Display results

## Player Actions

### Hit
- Request additional card to increase hand value
- Available when: hand < 21
- Risk: May cause hand to bust (exceed 21)

### Stand
- Keep current hand and proceed to dealer play
- Ends player's turn

### Double Down
- Double the initial bet and receive exactly 1 additional card
- Available when: hand value is 9, 10, or 11, and no cards have been drawn yet
- Strategy: Increases payout on strong starting hands
- Restricts to single card (cannot hit again after doubling)

### Split
- Divide a pair into two separate hands
- Available when: first two cards have equal value
- Each hand receives an additional card
- Each hand plays independently with original bet on each
- Restriction: Aces split into single card draws (no additional cards)

### Insurance (if enabled)
- Side bet against dealer blackjack
- Available when: dealer shows Ace
- Bet amount: 50% of original wager
- Payout: 2:1 if dealer has blackjack
- Loss: Bet amount if dealer doesn't have blackjack

## House Edge

The default ACE configuration implements:
- **House Edge: ~0.5-0.6%** (with basic strategy play)
- Based on standard Las Vegas rules
- Edge varies with specific rule variations

## Soft Hand Rules

A "soft hand" contains an Ace counted as 11 without busting.

**Examples:**
- Ace + 5 = Soft 16 (can count as 6 or 16)
- Ace + 7 = Soft 18 (can count as 8 or 18)

Soft hands allow additional strategic flexibility:
- Player can never bust with a hit on soft hand (worst case: Ace becomes 1)
- Dealer typically stands on soft 17 in ACE rules

## Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Upcard** | Dealer's visible card |
| **Hole Card** | Dealer's hidden card (revealed at end) |
| **Bust** | Hand value exceeds 21 |
| **Push** | Tie between player and dealer |
| **Stiff Hand** | Hand value 12-16 (high bust risk) |
| **Soft Hand** | Hand containing Ace counted as 11 |
| **Unit** | Standard betting denomination |
